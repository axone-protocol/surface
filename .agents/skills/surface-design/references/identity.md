# Identity

Identity is the fundamental institutional primitive of Surface.

Every institution, authority, resource or governed object is rooted in an Abstract Account.

## Identity represents institutional existence

An identity exists once an Abstract Account has been instantiated.

Identity precedes every institutional capability.

## Identity follows protocol

Identity is established by protocol facts.

Surface does not classify identities beyond what the protocol explicitly establishes.

## Principles

Identity is stable.

Identity is addressable.

Identity is inspectable.

Identity is composable.

## Representation

Identity is represented by:

- its canonical address;
- derived names when explicitly established;
- institutional capabilities;
- observable protocol activity.

## References

Compact canonical addresses MAY be used only when they are unique within the visible view. When an abbreviation collides, expand it until every displayed address is unique.
Every displayed identity MUST expose its full, copyable canonical address during inspection.
Derived names remain limited to those explicitly established by protocol facts.

## Capabilities

Capabilities are attached to identities.

Capabilities modify what an identity can institutionally perform.

Capabilities do not redefine the identity itself.

## Inspection

Inspection exposes:

- canonical address;
- installed modules;
- protocol activity;
- associated evidence.

Inspection progressively reveals technical details while preserving institutional reading.
